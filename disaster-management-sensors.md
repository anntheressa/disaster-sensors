Sensors Covered in This Notebook

This notebook discusses the most widely used sensors in disaster and emergency management, including:

1. Earthquake & Structural Monitoring Sensors
MEMS accelerometers
Geophones
Strain gauges
Tilt sensors (inclinometers)

2. Flood & Water-Related Sensors
Water level sensors (ultrasonic, radar, pressure-based)
Flow sensors (Doppler, mechanical)
Rain gauges

3. Fire, Wildfire & Heat Detection Sensors
Temperature sensors (thermistors, thermocouples)
Infrared flame sensors
Optical smoke / particulate (PM2.5 / PM10) sensors

4. Gas & Chemical Hazard Sensors
Electrochemical gas sensors
MQ-series gas sensors
NDIR (infrared) CO₂ sensors
VOC (volatile organic compound) sensors

5. Environmental Health Sensors
Humidity sensors
Air quality sensors (CO, O₃, NO₂, SO₂)
Barometric pressure sensors

6. Acoustic & Vibration Sensors
Microphones for landslides / structural noise
Acoustic event detectors (e.g., glass-break, gunshot detection)

7. Motion & Occupancy Sensors (Emergency Response)
PIR sensors



Ultrasonic motion sensors

Thermal imaging sensors (for locating trapped survivors)
